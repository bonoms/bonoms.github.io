My cause of concern

This text talks about a girl that is really concerned for his little brother Craig that is spending too much time in front of video games.

She talks about how he was a lovely boy, he used to play football, cycling and even martial arts but now he sped 4 hours a day in front of a screen.

Reaserches have shown that on average child spend 25 minutes on video games. In some cases this led to a problem, like in Craig case. Child that play video games are more likely to behave badly, for example play truant, stop doing homework, so on..

Those behave could become more problematic when child grow up and could get more violent. This is also a problem for your health, it’s not good to stay sat all day long, video games could cause also insomnia.

Researchers says that more experiment are needed but certainly there’s the evidence that video games use improperly aren’t good for physical and mental health.

The kindness of strangers

The first person says that he was in Iran, crossing the Persian Desert and it was very cold. He couldn’t feel his fingers because he was going on the scooter without gloves, and suddenly he saw that there was a car that stopped in front of him. The man driving the car got out and gave his gloves to him. Unfortunately, he couldn’t speak English so he never said thank you to the person who probably saved his fingers.

The second person says that when she was coming back from South Africa in 2003, she had to take The Tube but she went to the station in the rush hour, so there were a lot of people. Because of them, she couldn’t see the train arriving, but fortunately two young girls helped her: they stood on top of a thing to see the train and they found a place to sit down on it, making somebody stand up. She would love to thank them now.

The third person says that about 30 years ago, he went shopping in oxford street with his wife and their two sons aged four and six years old. They were in the first floor looking for clothes in a clothes shop and found out that his four-year-old kid was missing. Then, he started searching him in the building and out in the street, but there were no sign of him. His wife, calmer than him, phoned the police station and they discovered that a woman had seen the boy crying on the pavement and made the decision to take him to the police station. Unfortunately, the family couldn’t say thank you to her because she didn’t left any name or address.

The last person says that she recently lost her purse in a bus, and two young girls claimed it. Her daughter, Tamsin, lost her purse like her mom but fortunately a kind person picked it up, found the address on the driving licence and put it in an envelope that was sent to her. She would love to be able to say thank you to that person who didn’t behave like the young girls.

Sharing economy

This text talks about sharing economy.

We live in a consumer economy and this tends us to men us to buy more things. For example, a person uses the drill for 6 to 13 minutes during his life, it’s not crucial to have one and keep it in the wardrobe for 99% o his lifetime.

Before the arrival of the internet, sharing was not secure and it wasn’t handy, but now with the internet people can rent anything they use little. Uber and Airbnb are the perfect examples, with Airbnb you can rent rooms that you don’t use or the whole house, if, for example, you are by the sea, and you can also read the reviews of users, to trust more.

Also an increase in sharing offers economic benefits to both users and companies.

Sharing economy could bring a sense of community that has been lost over time.

Seoul is called the “city of sharing” because, for example, inside the flats there are libraries for residents and in this city people also lend cars or clothes each other.

Fact or fiction

  • The milk of a hippopotamus is pink: FACT, because in the milk there’s a red pigment that makes it pink.
  • Sal water boils quicker than fresh water: FACT, because when there’s salt in water it requires less energy to get to boil.
  • Ducks' quacks don’t echo: FICTION, many people believe this is a fact, however it a fiction. Maybe because their quack sound in’t loud enough to make an echo.
  • A toilet flush rotates in a different way depending on which hemisphere you are in: FACT, it happens because the Corriolis force, this makes the flush rotate differenly depending in which hemisphere you’re in.
  • Hurricanes always have girls' name: FICTION, because from 1950s to 1980s it was true, however now it has changed. There’s a name for each letter of the alphabet and many letter have a male name. For example GONZALO
  • Elephant can’t jump: FACT, it’s impossible for an elephant to jump because they have really heavy bones and no flexibility.
  • Too much sugar makes children hyperactive: FICTION, many people believe this is true but in reality it’s only a myth. People believe this maybe because they think sugar gives you too much energy.
  • Fish have a three second memory: FICTION, researches have proved that many fish have the capability to remember food taste much longer than 3 seconds, for example the gold fish can remember as long as 5 month.
